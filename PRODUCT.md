# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The product is for people who want a quiet, private place to record emotions, daily fragments, and later reflections. It should work first for an individual user, but the intended future audience is broader: multiple users can register, sign in, and keep their diary data separate and private.

Users may open it casually during the day or at night, often in a reflective mood. They need fast capture, low visual noise, and confidence that their private writing is protected.

## Product Purpose

小日常记 helps users record mood, tags, dates, images, comments, and personal reflections in a private diary timeline. Success means users feel comfortable returning every day because the interface is simple, calm, trustworthy, and never makes writing feel like filling out a database form.

The product should make privacy visible without becoming technical. It should support encryption and account isolation while keeping the writing experience lightweight.

## Brand Personality

Calm, personal, restrained.

The product should feel simple and beautiful with its own quiet character. It can borrow the clarity of iOS Notes, the calm structure of Notion, and the intimacy of a personal journal, but it should not imitate any one reference too closely.

## Anti-references

Do not make it feel like an admin dashboard, CMS, CRM, or developer tool. Avoid flashy decoration, generic templates, heavy gradients, loud cards, busy navigation, overly commercial SaaS styling, and anything that makes the diary feel performative instead of private.

Avoid visual systems that feel too cute, too corporate, too gamified, or too ornamental. The interface should not compete with the user's writing.

## Design Principles

1. Writing comes first: every screen should make recording a thought feel immediate and low-friction.
2. Privacy is calm confidence: communicate safety through clear flows and restrained copy, not warnings everywhere.
3. Emotion is a signal, not decoration: mood color should be subtle and useful.
4. Reveal complexity gradually: filters, export, comments, tags, and images should stay available without dominating the main timeline.
5. A diary should feel personal at scale: multi-user support should not make the product feel generic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-operable controls, touch targets of at least 44px, reduced-motion support, and color choices that do not rely on hue alone to communicate state.
